Methods of Catching Tuna

There are three main fishing methods used to capture tuna: purse seine, longline, and pole-and-line fishing.

Purse seine fishing involves deploying a large net capable of encircling and capturing thousands of tuna at once in the open ocean. Longline fishing uses long lines with baited hooks, extending into the open ocean to catch tuna individually. Pole-and-line fishing employs a hand-held wooden or fiberglass pole with a fishing line, used to catch fish one at a time.

For skipjack, purse seine fishing has consistently been the dominant method.

For bigeye, longline fishing was traditionally more common, but in recent years, purse seine and longline catches have become nearly equal.

Efficiency vs. Value

Purse seine fishing is significantly more efficient due to its ability to catch large quantities at once. For example, in 2023, a single purse seine operation captured an average of 35 skipjack, whereas a longline operation caught an average of 0.06 skipjack tuna a staggering 580 times less.

However, while purse seine fishing yields higher quantities, tuna caught via longline fishing are typically larger and of higher quality, making them more valuable, particularly for the sashimi market.
